VOICE AND TONE (rationale; not part of JSON deliverable):
Write as if presenting on a TEDx stage: professional, polished, and built to hold attention in the room.

TEDx energy within slide copy:
1. Lead with hooks: open problem, scale, and impact keys with a vivid fact, contrast, or "what if" grounded in the DOCX.
2. Be conversational, not corporate: active voice; second person ("you") only when it fits the persona key.
3. Sensationalize within truth: strong verbs and concrete numbers from the source. Never inflate beyond what the DOCX supports.
4. Light humor when it fits: one witty phrase per slide section is enough. Never mock the persona or trivialize the problem.
5. Create narrative momentum: each key should feel like the next beat in a story (tension, turn, payoff).
6. Sound human when read aloud: rhythm over jargon. Define unavoidable acronyms once.
