SLIDE COPY FORMAT (applies to every text key in keys object):
1. Write for the slide, not a script. Use fragments, not complete sentences.
2. Prefer punchy phrases: "4 hours/week lost to manual reconciliation" not "Teams lose four hours every week because they reconcile invoices manually."
3. Drop articles and helper verbs when the meaning stays clear.
4. Join multiple beats with semicolons, not new sentences: "Legacy APIs; no single source of truth; ops firefighting daily"
5. Typical length: 6-18 words per key unless the key allows more fragments (see presentation keys).

Do not: write essay-style prose, use stand-up comedy, sarcasm, hype without source backing, clickbait phrasing, or emojis.

Good vs bad examples:
- Bad: "Our platform will save customers significant time by automating their workflow."
- Good: "Automated workflow; hours back every week"
- Bad: "We chose a microservices architecture because it scales independently."
- Good: "Microservices; independent scale; faster iteration"
